# Analysis of Pandemic Disruption in Air Travel

This project analyzes how air travel patterns changed before, during, and after the COVID-19 pandemic. Using flight data from JFK Airport between 2019 and 2023, the project focuses on cancellations, delays, and route activity to understand how the pandemic disrupted airline operations and how travel patterns changed over time.

## Project Overview

The main research question was:

**How did the number of canceled flights from JFK differ before, during, and after the COVID-19 pandemic?**

To answer this, I examined changes in flight cancellations across multiple years and compared broader trends in delays and route activity. The goal was to measure the operational impact of the pandemic on air travel reliability and recovery patterns.

## Data Work

I cleaned and organized flight data from 2019 to 2023 and analyzed more than 10,000 records to compare patterns across time. ## Key Findings

- Flight cancellations changed significantly across the pre-pandemic, pandemic, and post-pandemic periods.
- During COVID-19 in 2021, cancellations were lower than in 2019, likely because fewer flights were scheduled overall.
- As travel resumed in 2022 and 2023, cancellation patterns returned closer to pre-pandemic levels, though slightly higher than before.
- The project found a 280% spike in cancellations during peak disruption periods, showing the pandemic’s effect on air travel reliability.
